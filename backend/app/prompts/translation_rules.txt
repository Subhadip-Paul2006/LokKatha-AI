TRANSLATION RULES FOR LOKKATHA AI:

1. English: Fluent, accessible prose. Preserve idioms by explaining their cultural meaning where needed.
2. Bengali: Use standard Bengali script (বাংলা). Maintain emotional tone and oral storytelling rhythm.
3. Hindi: Use Devanagari script (हिन्दी). Use respectful registers (आप/आदर) when referring to elders.

GENERAL GUIDELINES:
- Do not transliterate when a proper script translation exists.
- Keep folk terms, place names, and ritual names intact; add brief parenthetical explanation only when essential.
- Preserve the narrator's voice — do not over-formalise colloquial speech.
- For code-mixed speech (Hinglish, Banglish), normalise into each target language naturally.
- Translations must reflect the same factual content — no additions, no omissions of substance.
- If a phrase has no direct equivalent, keep the original term and provide the nearest cultural parallel.
