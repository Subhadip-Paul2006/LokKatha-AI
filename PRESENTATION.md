# LokKatha AI - Hackathon Pitch Deck Script

*This script is designed for a 3-minute live presentation.*

---

## Slide 1: The Problem (0:00 - 0:30)
**Visual**: A faded, crumbling Bengali manuscript alongside a modern smartphone.
**Speaker**: 
"India holds one of the world's richest oral traditions, but it is dying. Our cultural memory—stories like *Thakurmar Jhuli*, passed down for generations—is locked in decaying manuscripts or forgotten entirely by the digital generation. We are losing our roots because our folklore isn't accessible where today's generation lives: on their phones, interacting with AI."

## Slide 2: The Solution - LokKatha AI (0:30 - 1:00)
**Visual**: The beautiful LokKatha AI landing page with the RAG pipeline architecture diagram fading in.
**Speaker**: 
"Enter LokKatha AI. We have built India's first Living Cultural Memory archive. It is a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) platform powered by Google Gemma and Supabase. We didn't just build a chatbot; we built an anthropological preservation engine."

## Slide 3: Live Demo (1:00 - 2:00)
**Visual**: Live screen recording or live drive of the application.
**Speaker**: 
*(Action: Click the "Who is Lalkamal?" Demo Script button)*
"Watch how fast Gemma processes the request. It isn't hallucinating—it is pulling exactly from our Supabase vector database."
*(Action: Open the Story Preview Drawer)*
"Look at this transparency. We don't just give an answer. We cite the exact book, region, and characters. This is crucial for cultural preservation."
*(Action: Click the Narration Button)*
"And for accessibility, we've integrated native Text-to-Speech narration, bridging the gap between text and oral tradition."

## Slide 4: The Tech Stack & Resilience (2:00 - 2:30)
**Visual**: Judge Dashboard telemetry (Latency, Tokens, Vector Search Time).
**Speaker**: 
"Under the hood, we are doing Hybrid Search in under 50ms using `pgvector`. Our entire pipeline is edge-compatible. And we built it with 'Resilience Mode'—if the network drops, LokKatha AI gracefully degrades to serve offline archives. It is built to survive, just like the stories it tells."

## Slide 5: The Future (2:30 - 3:00)
**Visual**: A roadmap showing Voice AI, Crowdsourcing, and Regional maps.
**Speaker**: 
"Tomorrow, we plan to allow rural storytellers to speak directly into the app, automatically transcribing and embedding their local lore into the global vector database. LokKatha AI isn't just an app; it's a digital museum for the future."
